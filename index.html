MTB RIDE GUIDE:

What Kind Of Rider Are You?:

       A beginner typically rides on smooth, gentle trails, focusing on basic balance, braking, and shifting, and often dismounts for obstacles like roots or rocks. An intermediate rider confidently handles singletrack with moderate features, can maintain momentum, and understands proper body positioning, tackling small jumps and drops with increasing consistency. An advanced rider navigates steep, technical terrain (like sustained rocky sections or large drops) at speed, displays mastery over bike handling skills (such as cornering and jumping), and can execute complex maneuvers smoothly and safely.
